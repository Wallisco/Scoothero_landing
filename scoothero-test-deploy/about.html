Why we exist

Two sides of the same hard road

Last-mile delivery in South Africa is unpredictable, unregulated and unforgiving. It squeezes the people at both ends of every parcel.

For fleet owners

Rising fuel and maintenance costs, mounting traffic fines, breakdowns, roadside call-outs and accident admin eat away at margins that were thin to begin with. Keeping a petrol fleet profitable means fighting on every front at once — routes, repairs, safety, cash flow.

For drivers

Delivery drivers battle soaring petrol prices, vehicle rental costs, data costs and the cost of simply living — often earning per drop while carrying all the risk. When a bike breaks, the income stops. Many end up fixing their own bikes on street corners because there's nowhere else to turn.

The road here

How ScootHero came to be

The research

We started with the numbers

Extensive research into fleet economics kept landing on the same conclusion: petrol bikes are expensive to run, expensive to maintain, and the costs fall hardest on the people least able to absorb them. An alternative was needed.

The realisation

Electric was the answer — if the energy problem was solved

EVs were clearly right for last-mile work: fewer moving parts, a fraction of the running cost. But only if charging never took a driver off the road. That led us to battery swapping — and to solar — as the backbone of the model.

The turning point

The month that made this personal

We watched drivers repairing their own bikes on street corners because no support existed for them. Then, in a single month, six delivery drivers lost their lives on our roads. That was the moment this stopped being a business case and became a responsibility. A platform built to support and empower drivers was created.

The build

Solutions shaped around the driver

We built for the realities of the job: affordable vehicle rentals, fuel-cost elimination, data optimisation and hands-on maintenance support — designed with first-hand experience of what fleet managers and drivers actually face.

First release

80 bikes. 430 drivers. Proof.

Our first software release went live with 80 bikes and 430 drivers on the platform — real routes, real shifts, real feedback. Every lesson from that fleet is built into the bikes and the network we run today.

Today

A full ecosystem, scaling nationally

Two purpose-built EVs engineered for Southern African roads, a growing battery swap network rolling out in phases across the country, financing paths backed by Eqstra, and a driver marketplace connecting fleets to the riders who keep them moving.
